Overview 

VIDEFY.ai is an all-in-one AI video creation platform that takes a user from idea to finished video in one place: story and storyboard, visuals generation, voiceover, subtitles, editing, review, and export. 
We’re building a product that removes the chaos of tool-hopping and turns a messy 8-hour workflow into something closer to minutes. That requires strong engineering taste: clean architecture, performance thinking, reliability, and great UX through code. 
This exercise is how we find top engineers. You’ll tackle several complex problems similar to what we solve in Videfy, and it’s a chance to show strong judgment: correct solutions, clean code, and clear tradeoffs. If you enjoy working through complexity and 
ambiguity and turning it into something simple and reliable, you’ll do well here. 
What to do 
Select 2 items from the list below, implement them, and submit your solution. 
Challenge Items 
1) Add media to the timeline (Drag & Drop + Upload) 
Support adding image, video, and audio files from the local computer to the timeline 
using: 
● Drag & Drop onto the timeline
● Upload button on the timeline toolbar 
Acceptance criteria 
● Support common file types for image/video/audio (reasonable defaults). 
● When adding a video file: 
○ Create a video clip on a video track and an audio clip on an audio track. 
○ Both clips must start at the same time and stay aligned. 
○ If the video has no audio, only create the video clip. 
● If upload fails or a file type is unsupported, show a clear error message. 
2) Remove Silence (timeline button) 
Add a Remove Silence action on the timeline. 
Acceptance criteria 
● Applies to the selected audio clip. 
● Removes leading and trailing silence. 
● Does not modify other clips on the timeline. 
● Handles edge cases (no silence, very short clip, silence-only clip) without crashing. 
● Should be undoable if an undo system exists. 
3) Cut (Split) a timeline item 
Enable cutting a timeline item by clicking the Cut button and then clicking a timeline item. 
Acceptance criteria 
● Split the item into two clips at the cut position. 
● Works for video, audio, and image clips (use reasonable behavior for images). 
● Both resulting clips must preserve correct timing and durations. 
● Cutting must not break the rest of the timeline layout. 
4) Canvas item selection + move 
Allow selecting an item on the canvas and moving it by selecting the item and dragging it off the canvas and back onto the canvas. 
Acceptance criteria 
● Item can be selected and clearly shows a selection state. 
● Dragging updates position smoothly. 
● After drop, keep the item within canvas bounds (clamp or snap back). 
● No crashes on fast drag, repeated moves, or edge-case positions. 

Data & State 
You will need to determine a solution to store/manage the data/state for: 
● adding media (drag/drop + upload) 
● remove silence 
● cut 
● canvas selection 
● canvas movement
